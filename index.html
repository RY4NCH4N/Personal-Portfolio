I'm an aerospace engineering student at the University of Bath, driven by a passion for aircraft design and advanced propulsion systems. I'm drawn to the intersection of aerodynamics and structural engineering — the discipline of making things that fly both fast and light.

My biggest project to date is a tilt rotor aircraft concept, where I was involved in rotor system design, structural layout, and CAD modelling. I enjoy tackling complex multi-disciplinary problems and translating them into clean, manufacturable designs.

Outside of my studies I spend time experimenting with CFD, building scale models, and following developments in the eVTOL and advanced air mobility space.

Project Timeline

2026
High Speed Rotary Wing Air Vehicle — Tilt Rotor
University of Bath
▼
VTOLRotary WingAerodynamicsCADStructures

Designed and analysed a high-speed tilt rotor air vehicle concept as part of a group design project. The vehicle combines the hover capability of a helicopter with the cruise efficiency of a fixed-wing aircraft, exploring the aerodynamic and structural trade-offs of a tilting rotor configuration at high forward flight speeds.

Responsibilities included rotor blade geometry definition, aerodynamic performance estimation across the flight envelope, structural sizing of the rotor hub assembly, and CAD modelling of the full assembly in SOLIDWORKS.

2025
Machine Design
University of Bath
▼
Mechanical DesignFEASOLIDWORKS

Completed a machine design project focused on the analysis and specification of mechanical components under realistic loading conditions. Work included gear train design, shaft sizing, bearing selection, and fatigue life estimation.

Applied engineering standards and safety factor methodologies to produce a fully documented design package, including technical drawings and a written design report.

2025
Shower Chair — Product Design
University of Bath
▼
Product DesignCADUser ResearchPrototyping

Designed an ergonomic shower chair for elderly and mobility-impaired users as part of a product design module. The project followed a full design process from user research and requirements capture through to concept generation, evaluation, and detailed design.

Produced a fully resolved CAD model in SOLIDWORKS, a rendered presentation, and a structural analysis to validate the chair frame under worst-case load conditions.
